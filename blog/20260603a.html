SpeaksHalf

Người khôn ăn nói nửa chừng, để cho kẻ dại nửa mừng nửa lo.

This is a Vietnamese idiom that, whilst I'm not sure if it's real (though for its popularity, that doesn't really matter), really inspires me into building a game: SpeaksHalf.

SpeaksHalf is going to be a game where you write a sentence, the system strips parts of it, and then the next person guesses the original sentence and write another one based on your sentence. More precisely, these are the rules:

  1. A player writes something, a sentence, a paragraph, a masterpiece;
  2. The system strips parts (usually half) of it and pass it to the next player;
  3. They then guess what the original sentence is, based on the remaining words, and continue the story, conversation, or whatever the first one wrote;
  4. The cycle repeats until it reaches the first player again. Then, everyone can see the entire story, conversation, or whatever the first player wrote in the first place, the text which could have sounded better were the system not to embezzle words. (Yes, you heard it right, «embezzle words». I couldn't believe which kind of system would do that...)

This sounds like a cool game, doesn't it? Though, you can only play in private rooms. I planned to allow public rooms, but maybe that's for later.

The game is going to release before my birthday (15 September). Test version will also be released.

The new Ebola epidemic

I've covered the Hanta Outbreak in the previous entry. By now it has become less of a trending topic now. But I'm here to present another outbreak, no, an EPI[C]demic: 2026 Central Africa Ebola epidemic.

Apparently, Ebola has recently spread in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Uganda. The outbreak is caused by Bundibugyo ebolavirus, which, as its name and what it does suggest, can cause ebola disease in human. According to Wikipedia, «It is the 17th Ebola outbreak in DRC and only five months after the end of the previous outbreak». As of 1 June 2026, 6 people have recovered while 49 people died. The number of confirmed cases is 330.

Well, there have been many ebola outbreaks in the past. Similar to diseases caused by the hantaviruses, however, the Ebola outbreaks have had a very high mortality rate, which can be up to 90%.
